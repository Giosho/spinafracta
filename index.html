Oslo

Oslo, capital of Norway, is a city of culture, nature, and history. It is also the birthplace of the Norwegian black metal scene.

  • Ulver – Early trilogy: Bergtatt to Nattens Madrigal
  • Satyricon – up to Nemesis Divina
  • Gorgoroth – pre-2004
  • Akershus Fortress – historic medieval fortress
  • Vigeland Park – sculpture park with 200+ sculptures
  • Metal Record Shops – small but legendary spots for collectors
  • Munch Museum – famous for Edvard Munch's "The Scream"
  • Population: ~700,000
  • Located at the end of the Oslofjord
  • Host of annual Øya Festival (music + culture)
  • Known for snowy winters and scenic fjords

Stockholm

Stockholm, capital of Sweden, is known for its rich history, architecture, and vibrant metal scene. Spread across 14 islands, it combines urban life with stunning natural beauty.

  • Morbid – early demos with Dead
  • God Macabre – The Winterlong (1993)
  • Uncanny – Splinters from an Ever-Changing Face
  • Gamla Stan – historic old town with cobblestone streets
  • Vasa Museum – preserved 17th century ship
  • Death Metal Clubs – local venues for underground scene
  • Royal Palace – one of the largest palaces in Europe
  • Population: ~975,000
  • Built across 14 islands connected by 57 bridges
  • Famous for Nobel Prize ceremonies
  • Strong connection to tech startups and design
